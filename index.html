What are we working on?

Physical Design

Our innovation is an ankle splint that is inflated and cooled by chemical reactions.

It has a front zipper to easily slip the foot inside the splint, and an outer inflatable layer. Inside, next to the ankle joining and limb is a thinner layer filled with the components for our chemical reaction to inflate the splint.

This thinner layer will also have many miniscule holes in order for gas to escape the inflate the splint while the liquid is contained because of capillary action.

The splint's chemical reactants will be in the shape of alternating parallellograms to allow for the Miura Fold to save space.

Chemical Reaction

REMINDER FOR KEVIN TO MAKE A CHEMICAL REACTION VIDEO FOLLOW THIS EXAMPLE

The chemical reaction uses solid Sodium Bicarbonate and aqueous Citric Acid to produce Trisodium Citrate, Water, and Carbon Dioxide gas. All reactants and products in this reaction are non-toxic and can be easily acquired. The Reaction is inherently endothermic which means it draws in heat from its surroundings to make it cold. This reduction in temperature combats excess inflamation that can provide discomfort and other complications within traditional splints. On the other hand, our reaction was carefully chosen as a too sharp decrease in temperature can result in shock and be detrimental for the patient.
